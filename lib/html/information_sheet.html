INFORMATION SHEET

Information for participants

Thank you for being willing to participate in our experiment. Your contribution to our research is very valuable and we appreciate your help.

What are these experiments about?

We are interested in understanding how people make decisions. In particular, we are interested in how people use knowledge that they have gained from past experience and how they apply it to new situations.

Who is conducting these experiments?

The experiments are being conducted by Prof Christopher Summerfield of the University of Oxford, England. The team can be contacted by e-mail on neuronoodle@gmail.com

What if I have a question?

We welcome questions about the project. If you have any questions, please email Dr Summerfield on neuronoodle@gmail.com

What will we ask you to do?

You will read some instructions before starting the experiment. We will also give you a reminder of what to do when the experiment starts.

Will these experiments reveal anything about my personality?

No. The data we get from you will be pooled with the data from other participants. That is because we are looking for trends that only emerge when a lot of data are analyzed. In any case, we can't infer anything about your individual personality from these data.

How will my data be used?

All we are recording is what key you press (or where you click on the screen) while you are performing the task, and also at what time relative to the start of the experiment. We collect no personal data other than that. At the end of each block, we save your data in a temporary file. When you get to the end of the experiment, the data will be pooled with those of many other participants. We will then use the pooled data to test our theories about learning and decision making. Only the members of the Summerfield laboratory stated above have access to your data. At the end of the project the data will be archived in case a query about the research arises after the results of the project are analysed and published.

What are the risks and benefits of the research?

There are no risks associated with the research. If successful, the project will hopefully result in a paper being published, so that other scientists can build on our work. In other words, it will help everyone understand better how people learn and make decisions.

What should I do if I have a complaint?

This project has been reviewed by, and received ethics clearance through, the University of Oxford Central University Research Ethics Committee. If you have a concern about any aspect of this project, please send an email to neuronoodle@gmail.com indicating which project you have participated in. We will do our best to answer your query. If you remain unhappy and wish to make a formal complaint, please contact Research Ethics Committee at the University of Oxford (ethics@medsci.ox.ac.uk; Medical Sciences Inter-Divisional Research Ethics Committee, Medical Sciences Divisional Office, Level 3 John Radcliffe Hospital, Oxford, OX3 9DU, UK

What if I don't like the experiment once I have started?

You can stop any time just by closing the program. However, you won't receive a verification code and thus we won't be able to reimburse you for your effort.
